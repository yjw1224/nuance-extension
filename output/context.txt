TERM DISAMBIGUATION
- Term: Mining / Miner
  - Meaning here: PoW process and actors that run specialized hardware to find block solutions.
  - Incorrect: “mining” as physical resource extraction.
  - Korean: 채굴 / 채굴자
- Term: Proof-of-Work
  - Meaning: Consensus algorithm requiring computational puzzle-solving.
  - Incorrect: Any generic “work” or effort.
  - Korean: 작업증명 (PoW) — first occurrence: 작업증명(PoW)
- Term: Proof-of-Stake
  - Meaning: Consensus where validators are chosen proportionally to coins they lock as stake.
  - Incorrect: “stake” as mere ownership without locking; “mining” process.
  - Korean: 지분증명 (PoS) — first occurrence: 지분증명(PoS)
- Term: Stake
  - Meaning: Coins locked/deposited as collateral to be chosen as validator; financial bond that can be slashed.
  - Incorrect: simple ownership, or stake as vote share without deposit.
  - Korean: 스테이크(예치금)/지분(예치금). Use “지분(예치금)” when formal; “스테이크” acceptable in casual parts but define once.
- Term: Validator
  - Meaning: PoS actor who validates and signs blocks (not “miner”).
  - Incorrect: salesperson/agent.
  - Korean: 검증자
- Term: Mint / Forge
  - Meaning: PoS term for creating a new block.
  - Incorrect: literal minting of coins only.
  - Korean: 블록 민팅(생성) 또는 블록 생성 — pick one consistently (recommend “블록을 생성(민팅)”)
- Term: Hash rate
  - Meaning: Computational throughput for mining.
  - Incorrect: hash as general data.
  - Korean: 해시레이트 / 해시율 (use 해시레이트)
- Term: Mining pool
  - Meaning: Grouping of miners to combine hash power and share rewards.
  - Incorrect: online forum group.
  - Korean: 채굴 풀
- Term: 51% attack
  - Meaning: Controlling majority of validation/mining power to double-spend or censor.
  - Incorrect: generic majority influence.
  - Korean: 51% 공격
- Term: Coin age based selection
  - Meaning: PoS selection scheme that factors how long coins have been staked.
  - Incorrect: coin market age or launch age.
  - Korean: 코인 연령 기반 선택

TRANSLATION RULES
- Use standard Korean crypto terminology (작업증명, 지분증명, 채굴, 검증자, 채굴 풀, 트랜잭션 수수료).
- Keep proper names and project names untranslated (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Cardano, Casper, Ouroboros, Peercoin, Lisk, Nxt, Digiconomist). If needed, append Korean pronunciation in parentheses only once.
- First mention: spell out Korean term + English acronym (예: 지분증명(PoS)). Afterwards use Korean term only.
- Translate “miners” → 채굴자; “validators” → 검증자; never swap them.
- Translate “mint/forge” as “블록 생성(민팅)” and use “생성” in casual sentences.
- Preserve numeric values, examples, and ratios exactly (e.g., $100 vs $1000, 54 TWh, 79 billion dollars).
- Use gender-neutral phrasing (“해당 노드/검증자”) instead of “he/she”.
- Prefer natural Korean idiomatic phrasing over literal word-for-word translation.
- Maintain consistency: once a Korean term chosen, reuse it throughout.

POTENTIAL TRAPS
- “Mining” vs everyday “mining”: ensure it’s translated as 채굴 (블록체인) not 광산 채굴.
- “Mint/forge” — unfamiliar to general viewers: mark as “블록 생성(민팅)” first time.
- “Stake” ambiguity: clarify as 예치금/담보 that can be 슬래시(벌금) (slashed).
- “Economies at scale” → translate as 규모의 경제 (meaning price/efficiency gains with scale).
- “More decentralized” claim: video argues PoS can encourage decentralization due to lower node cost — translate as “더 분산화될 수 있다(가능성)” not absolute.
- “Sign off on the block” → means cryptographic signature/approval, not casual approval; translate as “서명하고 블록에 추가/확정”.
- “Release after a certain period” — refer to unstaking delay; translate as 출금 지연(언스테이킹 락업) to avoid implying immediate withdrawal.

Keep the tone conversational-educational in Korean subtitles, but precise where technical distinctions matter.
